# Acme Corp Travel Reimbursement Policy

## General Principles

All business travel must be pre-approved by the employee's direct manager. Reimbursement claims must be submitted within 30 days of trip completion. Original receipts are required for all expenses exceeding $25. The company reimburses only reasonable and necessary business expenses. Employees are expected to exercise the same care in incurring expenses as a prudent person traveling on personal business.

## Flight Policy

Economy class is the standard for all domestic flights. Business class is permitted for international flights exceeding 8 hours with manager pre-approval. Maximum reimbursable airfare is $800 for domestic and $2,500 for international flights. Flights should be booked at least 14 days in advance when possible. Budget airlines and advance-purchase fares are encouraged. First class travel is not reimbursable unless medically necessary with documentation.

## Hotel Policy

Standard hotel rooms at or below the company per-diem rate are reimbursable. Domestic per-diem hotel rate is $200 per night. International per-diem hotel rate is $350 per night. Extended stays of 5 or more nights should use negotiated corporate rates. Room upgrades, resort fees, and minibar charges are not reimbursable. Cancellation fees are reimbursable only when caused by business schedule changes approved by management.

## Meals Policy

Daily meal allowance is $75 per day for all travel. Individual meal maximum is $50 (any single meal exceeding this requires justification). Alcohol is not reimbursable under any circumstances. Team dinners with 4 or more team members may exceed the individual per-diem with manager approval up to $100 per person. Tips are included in the meal reimbursement and should not exceed 20% of the meal cost. Room service is reimbursable within the daily meal allowance.

## Ground Transportation Policy

Rideshare and taxi expenses are reimbursable up to $100 per day. Rental cars should be mid-size category or below; SUV or luxury vehicles require VP approval. Personal vehicle mileage is reimbursed at $0.67 per mile up to 500 miles per trip. Parking fees are reimbursable with receipt up to $50 per day. Public transportation is always reimbursable with receipt.

## Conference and Event Expenses

Registration fees are fully reimbursable with pre-approval documentation. Conference materials and required supplies are reimbursable up to $200. Networking events at conferences are reimbursable up to $100 per event. All conference expenses require pre-approval from the employee's manager before the event.

## Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are never reimbursable: personal entertainment (movies, spa, gym), alcohol purchases, clothing or personal items, traffic violations or parking tickets, travel insurance (company provides coverage), upgrades beyond policy class unless medically necessary, expenses for accompanying family members, minibar charges, laundry for trips under 5 days, and personal phone calls.

## Miscellaneous Expenses

Incidental expenses such as tips for porters, phone charges for business calls, and small office supplies are reimbursable up to $150 per trip total. Internet access fees at hotels are reimbursable. International phone plan add-ons for business purposes are reimbursable.

## Approval Thresholds

Claims up to $500 require direct manager approval. Claims from $501 to $2,000 require department director approval. Claims from $2,001 to $5,000 require VP approval. Claims exceeding $5,000 require CFO approval. International travel requires additional travel desk sign-off regardless of amount.

## Documentation Requirements

All claims must include trip dates, business purpose, and itemized expenses. Receipts are required for individual expenses over $25. Missing receipts for expenses between $25 and $75 may be covered by a signed employee declaration. Missing receipts for expenses over $75 require manager exception approval. Foreign currency expenses should be converted at the exchange rate on the date of the expense.

## Duplicate and Fraud Prevention

Submitting the same expense on multiple claims is grounds for disciplinary action including termination. The system flags claims with identical amounts, dates, and vendors across submissions. Random audits are conducted quarterly by the finance team. Intentional misrepresentation of expenses will result in immediate termination and potential legal action.
